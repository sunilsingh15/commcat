Who are our community cats?

Community cats are cats that reside in public spaces across Singapore. Cats are very territorial and generally roam within a fixed neighbourhood, especially when they are cared for by volunteer feeders!

An easy way to identify a community cat is to check if its left ear has been tipped. This signifies that the cat has been sterilised, most likely as part of the 'Trap-Neuter-Return-Manage' Programme.

Are they friendly?

It's tough to say!

All cats have different personalities, which include how friendly they are towards humans. If you wish to befriend a cat, we recommend you let them approach and sniff you first.

If they show signs of fear or aggression, like running away, hissing or hiding, you should give them their space and leave them alone.

Can I feed them?

If you wish to feed your community cat, please do so responsibly!

If a cat looks like it's well-fed, it probably has a regular feeder. By choosing to feed the cat, you may disrupt its regular feeding schedule, and even contribute to overfeeding.

If you're not sure if a cat has a regular feeder, try asking around in relevant community Facebook groups!

When feeding a community cat, please use containers (e.g. a plastic bowl) for food and water. Feeding food directly from a can is dangerous as they can cut their tongue on the sharp edges.

Placing food directly on the ground is unhygienic and can attract pests.

Once the cat is fed, please clear up the remaining food and/or water immediately! Leaving food out for longer than an hour can cause it to go stale and attract pests. Leaving water out may cause mosquito breeding.

By failing to clean up, you may also be committing a crime by littering the environment!
